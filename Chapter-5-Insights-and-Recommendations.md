# Key Insights

### High Appointment No-Show Rate (26%)
Approximately 26% of scheduled appointments result in no-shows. This represents a substantial loss of clinical capacity, as nearly one in four time slots does not result in completed care. This inefficiency may contribute to lost revenue, underutilized staff time, and reduced system productivity. It also suggests potential gaps in patient engagement or appointment reminder processes.

### Visit Demand is Driven Primarily by Routine Check-Ups
The most common reason for patient visits is routine check-ups, indicating that a large portion of system demand is preventive rather than acute or specialized care. This suggests that operational planning (staffing, scheduling, and resource allocation) should prioritize high-volume, low-complexity visits.

### Revenue is Concentrated in X-Ray Services
X-ray treatments generate the highest total revenue among all treatment types. This indicates that imaging services play a major role in overall financial performance.


# Recommendations

### Reduce Appointment No-Show Rate

With a no-show rate of approximately 26%, there is a significant opportunity to improve appointment attendance. The organization should consider strengthening patient engagement strategies such as automated reminders, confirmation systems, or flexible rescheduling options. Reducing missed appointments would increase clinical efficiency, improve revenue capture, and reduce wasted provider time.

### Optimize Scheduling Around High-Frequency Visit Types

Since routine check-ups represent the most common visit reason, scheduling systems should prioritize capacity for high-volume, low-complexity visits. This may include allocating dedicated time blocks or provider schedules specifically for check-ups to reduce bottlenecks and improve patient flow.

### Strengthen Revenue Strategy Around High-Performing Services

X-ray services generate the highest total revenue, indicating strong demand and financial importance. The organization should ensure that imaging capacity is adequately supported through staffing, equipment availability, and scheduling efficiency. Maintaining strong performance in this area is critical to sustaining overall revenue.
